Abstract

Our primary question is: What are the main factors contributing to automobile accidents in Maryland?. Understanding these factors is important and crucial because they can guide policies to enhance road safety. To address this question, we will utilize an extensive dataset from the Maryland State Polices Automated Crash Reporting System (ACRS), which will help us identify and analyze these factors.

Keywords

Road Safety, Automobile Accidents, Crash Factors Analysis, ACRS.

Introduction

Briefly introduce your project. Include 3-5 research questions. What motivates the questions? Why are they important? (at least 200 words)

Our project will be focusing on automobile accidents in the state of Maryland. We want to effectively analyze the contributing factors, conditions, and patterns that lead to traffic collisions on county and local roadways. This data set includes characteristics of accidents reported by callers/police such as date, weather, surface condition, (driver) substance abuse, injury severity, distraction, speed limit, and vehicle model and make; all of which will help us formulate important research questions. Some of these questions include:

What are the main contributing factors to automobile accidents in Maryland, and how do they vary across different counties and local roadways? How does weather impact the frequency and severity of traffic collisions, and are there specific weather conditions that pose a higher risk? Are there any notable patterns or correlations between the vehicle model and make and the severity of accidents, and could certain vehicle types be associated with higher risk? Was there an increase in automobile accidents in relation to driver substance abuse and/or age of drivers after the pandemic? The motivation behind these questions is to gain a comprehensive understanding of the conditions and circumstances that lead to automobile accidents in Maryland. Analyzing this data and finding patterns can help lawmakers and law enforcement agencies better strategize ways to enhance road safety through education and intervention.

The Dataset

Where did you find the data? Please include a link to the data source

We found the data in data.gov database. Here is a link to our data source: https://catalog.data.gov/dataset/crash-reporting-drivers-data

Who collected the data?

The Maryland State police collected the data through the ARCS.

How was the data collected or generated?

The crashes were collected through the Automated Crash Reporting System (ACRS) of the Maryland State Police. It was reported by the Montgomery County Police, Gaithersburg Police, Rockville Police, and the Maryland-National Capital Park Police based on preliminary information supplied to the Police Department by the reporting parties.

Why was the data collected?

This data was collected with the intent for public access and use. It conveys important information about the characteristics and frequencies of car incidents in their community.

How many observations (rows) are in your data?

There are 162992 observations (rows) in our data.

How many features (columns) are in the data?

There are 43 features (columns) in the data.

What, if any, ethical questions or questions of power do you need to consider when working with this data?

There are ethical questions concerning fairness, accuracy, and privacy of this data. Since the reporting parties of the incidents are supplying this information, certain details may be skewed, inaccurate, and not verified as those involved may be emotional, injured, or biased towards a certain cause at fault. These reports are also only reflective of preliminary crash information that may/may not be updated based on further investigations, as well as mechanical and human error. This data is also available for the public and its concerning if people want their car crash information out in the public.

What are possible limitations or problems with this data? (at least 200 words)

Possible limitations with this data is that it only provides car incidents in the state of Maryland so it cannot be generalized for all of the United States of America. Theres also no context leading up to the incident so we cannot categorize if maybe some incidents are due to fatigue and others are due to alcohol, etc. Additionally, we can not categorize if the car crash is a fatal/serious one or just a minor one like a rear end. Since the dataset is so large, I think it is important to know the extend of the damage so it does not seem like Maryland just has a lot of fatal car crashes. There could also be underreporting as the crashes are reported into an online system, which sometimes a minor crash may not be reported as sometimes people dont always get the police involved and resolve it between themselves. There could potentially be data quality issues sense there are those specific 43 features listed, maybe some crashes need more information and could have a not detailed enough report if the crash was more serious. There could potentially be human error when recording this data since it is the police who are recording the crashes into the ACRS and they could potentially input their data wrong, resulting in potential skews, or outliers in the data.

Limitations & Challenges

What challenges or limitations might you need to address with your project idea more broadly? Briefly discuss. (at least 150 words)

A challenge we might need to address is the how much data we have available. The data we have only records car crashes in the state of Maryland. With this information in mind, we can not generalize the data or conclusions we come to and say it applies to all of America as the data collected can only represent the state of Maryland. Another challenge we might to consider are external factors or context that might influence the data. The car accident could be influenced by external factors such as poor weather such as snowstorms or the COVID pandemic, both of which could increase the likelihood of an crash occurring or make the accident more severe. Additionally, another challenge could be if the conclusions or trends we discover with this data could be applied to future situations such as can we predict if the number of accidents is going to increase or if the number of accidents in a specific area occurs the most and if we can resolve/decrease the amount of crashes in that location.
